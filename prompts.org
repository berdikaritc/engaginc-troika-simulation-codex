** Layout of the screen & appearance

- The top logo+information area: the very top will contain title (use logo file
  logo/logo.png), and turn/step information, and nothing else. The logo

  screen. The cards put in this hand should not overlap or cover the top area or
  the discard pile.
- discard pile: discard pile which will also occupy about 1/3 of vertical area
  of the screen. The cards put in this pile should not overlap or cover the
  cards in P1's hand or P2's hand.
- P2's hand: P2's hand which will also occupy about 1/3 of vertical area of the

The players' hands will be displayed in accordeon style with fixed spacing between

and subsequent cards on the right are cards that are on top of the previous
cards (cards to their left).

The discard pile will be displayed in accordeon style with initial spacing
between cards of ~1/6 width of the card, but as more cards are put into the
discard pile, this spacing will be reduced gradually. When there are 12 to 19
cards on the discard pile, the spacing is reduced to ~1/8 width of the card.
When there are 20 or more cards on the discard pile, the spacing is reduced to
~1/10 width of the card.

** The simulation data
An example simulation data is in simdata/S1/simulation.json file. The simulation
data is represented by a JSON object.

The simulation JSON object specifies list of turns to make (in its 'turns'
property, which is an array of turns). Each turn is a JSON object containing
information of the number of steps to make (in its 'steps' property, which is an
array of steps). Each step is a JSON object containing 'summary' as well as list
of card moves to make (in its 'moves' property, which is an array of moves).
Each card move is a 3-element array in the format of [CARDNAME, FROM_PILE,
TO_PILE] specifying movement of card from a pile to another pile.

There are 4 different "piles" in the game:
- P1: P1's hand, which will be shown in an accordeon style to make sure the top
  left part of each card is visible to let viewers see the symbols on the card
  and know what card it is).
- P2: P2's hand.
- DRAW: the draw pile, which is not shown in the display, but when a player is
  drawing from the draw pile, the card will be animated from the left of the
  screen and vertically middle area to the P1's hand.
- DISCARD: the discard pile, which is also shown in an accordeon style to let
  viewers identify each card.

The first turn consists of one step, which consists of 18 card moves: drawing 9
cards to each player (first card to P1, then second card to P2, then third card
to P1, and so on). Note that the movement of cards is ordered, so subsequent
card moved to the same target pile will be on top of the previously moved card .

Subsequent turns will each consist of 4 separate steps. First step is P1
discarding a group of cards from his hand to the discard pile and call his
discarded card group. Second, P1 draws another 3 card from the draw pile. Third,
P2 continues by discarding a group of cards from his hand to the discard pile and
call his discarded card group. Fourth, P2 draws 3 cards from the draw pile.

When it is the turn of a player (e.g. it's P1's turn for turn 2, P2's turn for

When animating a player discarding his group of cards, first all the cards that
